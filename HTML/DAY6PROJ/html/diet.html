Diet Essentials

Transform your body with science-backed nutrition tailored to your goals. Discover macro breakdowns, meal plans, tools, and recipes to fuel muscle gain, fat loss, or sustainable health.

Macro Breakdown

Proteins

Essential for muscle repair and growth. Aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight daily to support muscle preservation and satiety.

  • Chicken breast: 31g protein/100g
  • Eggs: 13g protein/100g
  • Greek yogurt: 10g protein/100g
  • Plant-based: Tofu (8g/100g), Lentils (9g/100g), Seitan (25g/100g)

Tip: Spread protein intake across meals (20-30g per meal) to optimize muscle synthesis. Use plant-based combos like beans and rice for complete proteins.

Carbohydrates

Primary energy source for workouts. Target 3-6g/kg body weight for active individuals, focusing on complex carbs for sustained energy.

  • Sweet potatoes: 20g carbs/100g
  • Oats: 66g carbs/100g
  • Quinoa: 21g carbs/100g
  • Fruits: Bananas (23g/100g), Berries (8-12g/100g, low-carb option)

Tip: Consume carbs 1-2 hours pre-workout and within 30 min post-workout for glycogen replenishment.

Fats

Support hormone production and nutrient absorption. Aim for 0.8-1.2g/kg body weight, prioritizing healthy fats.

  • Avocado: 15g fat/100g
  • Nuts: Almonds (49g/100g), Walnuts (65g/100g)
  • Fish: Salmon (13g/100g, rich in Omega-3s)
  • Oils: Olive oil (14g fat/tbsp), Coconut oil (moderate use)

Tip: Pair fats with fiber-rich foods to enhance satiety. Avoid trans fats and limit processed oils.

Meal Prep Guide

Save time and stay consistent with these meal prep tips:

  • Batch Cook: Prepare proteins (chicken, tofu) and carbs (rice, quinoa) for 3-5 days.
  • Portion Control: Use containers to pre-measure meals for accurate macros.
  • Storage: Refrigerate for up to 4 days; freeze for longer storage.
  • Variety: Rotate recipes weekly to avoid burnout and ensure nutrient diversity.
  • Tools: Invest in a food scale and quality containers for precision.

Nutrition FAQs

How do I balance macros for my goals?

Use our calculator to estimate needs. Muscle gain: 30-35% protein, 40-50% carbs, 15-25% fats. Fat loss: 35-40% protein, 30-40% carbs, 20-30% fats. Track and adjust weekly.

Can I follow a vegan diet for fitness?

Yes! Combine plant-based proteins (lentils, tofu, seitan) for complete amino acids. Supplement with B12, iron, and consider vegan protein powders.

How much water should I drink?

Aim for 3-4L daily, more if active. Hydration supports performance and recovery.

Are supplements necessary?

Not mandatory, but whey protein, creatine (3-5g/day), and multivitamins can fill gaps. Consult a nutritionist for personalized advice.
