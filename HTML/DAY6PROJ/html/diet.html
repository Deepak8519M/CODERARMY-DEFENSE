Diet Essentials

Fuel your body with balanced nutrition to maximize gains, boost energy, and accelerate recovery. Our guide combines science-backed advice with practical meal ideas for sustainable results.

Understand Your Macros

Proteins

Essential for muscle repair and growth. Aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight daily. High-protein diets can help tame hunger and support weight loss while preserving muscle.

  • Chicken breast: 31g protein/100g
  • Eggs: 13g protein/100g
  • Greek yogurt: 10g protein/100g
  • Plant-based: Tofu (8g/100g), Lentils (9g/100g)

Tips: Include protein in every meal. For vegetarians, combine sources like beans and rice for complete proteins.

Carbohydrates

Fuel for high-intensity workouts. Focus on complex carbs for sustained energy. Balance with veggies and fruits for fiber and nutrients.

  • Sweet potatoes: 20g carbs/100g
  • Oats: 66g carbs/100g
  • Brown rice: 23g carbs/100g
  • Fruits: Bananas (23g/100g), Berries (low-carb option for weight loss)

Tips: Time carbs around workouts for optimal performance. Limit refined carbs to avoid blood sugar spikes.

Fats

Support hormone production and joint health. Opt for healthy sources like omega-3s. Avoid trans fats and limit saturated fats.

  • Avocados: 15g fat/100g
  • Nuts: Almonds (49g/100g)
  • Fish: Salmon (13g/100g, rich in Omega-3s)
  • Oils: Olive oil for cooking

Tips: Fats are calorie-dense, so portion control is key for weight loss. Include them for satiety.

Focus on surplus calories for muscle gain. Adjust based on your needs.

Maintain weight while building muscle. Include all food groups.

Pro Tips for Success

  • Eat a variety of nutrient-rich foods; limit added sugars and fried items.
  • Personalize: Calculate daily needs based on age, weight, activity level.
  • Pre/Post Workout: Carbs before for energy, protein after for recovery.
  • Supplements: Consider whey protein or multivitamins if diet lacks.
  • Track Progress: Use apps like MyFitnessPal for accountability.
