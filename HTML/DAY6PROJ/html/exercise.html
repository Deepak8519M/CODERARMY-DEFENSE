Exercise Routines

Build strength, endurance, and confidence with tailored workouts for all levels. From full-body splits to targeted routines, these plans are designed for progressive overload and recovery.

Beginner Level

Focus on form and consistency. 3 days/week full-body routine to build foundation.

Day Exercises (Sets x Reps)
Day 1: Full Body Squats (3x10), Push-ups (3x8), Rows (3x10), Plank (3x20s)
Day 2: Rest/Cardio 30 min walk or light jog
Day 3: Full Body Lunges (3x8/leg), Overhead Press (3x10), Deadlifts (3x8), Crunches (3x15)
Rest Days Stretch and recover

Tips: Start with bodyweight; add dumbbells as you progress. Warm-up 5-10 min.

Intermediate Level

Increase intensity with 4-day split. Incorporate weights and cardio.

Day Exercises (Sets x Reps)
Day 1: Push Bench Press (4x8), Shoulder Press (4x10), Tricep Dips (4x12)
Day 2: Pull Pull-ups (4x6), Rows (4x10), Bicep Curls (4x12)
Day 3: Legs Squats (4x8), Lunges (4x10/leg), Calf Raises (4x15)
Day 4: Cardio/Core HIIT 20 min, Planks/Crunches
Rest Days Active recovery like yoga

Tips: Use progressive overload—increase weights 5-10% weekly. Include mobility work.

Advanced Level

5-6 days/week with specialization. Focus on compound lifts and HIIT.

Day Exercises (Sets x Reps)
Day 1: Chest/Back Bench (5x5), Deadlifts (5x5), Pull-ups (5x8)
Day 2: Legs Squats (5x5), Leg Press (4x10), Hamstring Curls (4x12)
Day 3: Shoulders/Arms Overhead Press (4x8), Curls (4x10), Tricep Extensions (4x12)
Day 4: Full Body Compound circuit: Squats, Push-ups, Rows (3 rounds)
Day 5: Cardio/HIIT 45 min intense cardio or intervals
Rest Days 1-2 days for full recovery

Tips: Track lifts; deload every 4-6 weeks. Combine with diet for recomposition.

Pro Tips for Success

  • Prioritize form to prevent injury; warm-up and cool-down always.
  • Progressive overload: Increase reps/weights gradually.
  • Balance strength, cardio, flexibility for overall fitness.
  • Rest: Sleep 7-9 hours; include active rest days.
  • Track: Use apps or journals; adjust based on progress.
