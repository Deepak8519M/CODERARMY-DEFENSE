Exercise Routines

Power up your fitness with tailored workouts for all levels. From strength to endurance, our plans ensure progressive overload, proper form, and recovery for maximum results.

Beginner: Build Your Base

Focus on form and consistency with a 3-day/week full-body routine to establish a strong foundation.

Day Exercises (Sets x Reps)
Day 1: Full Body Bodyweight Squats (3x12), Push-ups (3x10), Dumbbell Rows (3x10), Plank (3x30s)
Day 2: Rest/Cardio 30 min brisk walk or cycling
Day 3: Full Body Lunges (3x10/leg), Shoulder Press (3x12), Deadlifts (3x10), Bicycle Crunches (3x15)
Rest Days Stretch, foam roll, or light yoga

Tip: Start light, focus on technique. Warm-up for 5-10 min before each session.

Intermediate: Level Up

Push intensity with a 4-day split, combining weights and cardio for strength and stamina.

Day Exercises (Sets x Reps)
Day 1: Push Bench Press (4x8), Overhead Press (4x10), Tricep Dips (4x12)
Day 2: Pull Pull-ups (4x8), Barbell Rows (4x10), Bicep Curls (4x12)
Day 3: Legs Squats (4x8), Romanian Deadlifts (4x10), Calf Raises (4x15)
Day 4: Cardio/Core HIIT (20 min sprints), Russian Twists (3x20)
Rest Days Active recovery with mobility work

Tip: Increase weights by 5-10% weekly for progressive overload. Include dynamic stretches.

Advanced: Elite Performance

Maximize gains with a 5-6 day/week program focusing on compound lifts, HIIT, and specialization.

Day Exercises (Sets x Reps)
Day 1: Chest/Back Bench Press (5x5), Deadlifts (5x5), Pull-ups (5x8)
Day 2: Legs Squats (5x5), Leg Press (4x10), Hamstring Curls (4x12)
Day 3: Shoulders/Arms Overhead Press (4x8), Bicep Curls (4x10), Tricep Extensions (4x12)
Day 4: Full Body Compound Circuit: Squats, Push-ups, Rows (3 rounds)
Day 5: Cardio/HIIT 45 min intense cardio or intervals
Rest Days 1-2 days for full recovery; foam roll

Tip: Track lifts in a journal. Deload every 4-6 weeks to prevent burnout.

Exercise FAQs

How often should I change my routine?

Every 4-8 weeks to avoid plateaus, but adjust based on progress. Focus on progressive overload by increasing weight or reps.

What’s the best way to prevent injuries?

Prioritize proper form, warm-up for 5-10 min, and include mobility work. Don’t increase weights too quickly.

How much rest do I need?

7-9 hours of sleep nightly and 1-2 rest days per week. Active recovery (yoga, stretching) aids muscle repair.
